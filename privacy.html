Privacy Policy

ChemRef — Compound Tracker for Microsoft Word
Last updated: March 31, 2026

Overview

ChemRef is a Microsoft Word add-in developed by Jinlong Tan. This policy describes how ChemRef handles your data.

Data Collection

ChemRef does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data or document content on external servers. The add-in operates entirely within Microsoft Word. All compound data — including structure images, IUPAC names, compound codes, and attached structure files — is stored exclusively inside the Word document itself as a Custom XML Part. No data ever leaves your document.

No Analytics or Telemetry

ChemRef does not include any analytics, tracking, telemetry, or usage monitoring of any kind. No cookies are set. No network requests are made by the add-in beyond loading the add-in's own interface files from the hosting server.

No External Services

ChemRef does not connect to any third-party services, APIs, databases, or cloud storage. It does not send any data to the developer or any other party.

Document Content

ChemRef reads and writes content within your Word document in order to provide its compound tracking functionality. This includes inserting and updating content controls, reading document text for consistency checks and unlinked reference scanning, and storing compound dictionary data in the document's Custom XML. All of this activity occurs locally within the Word application.

Data Sharing

Because ChemRef stores compound data inside the Word document, sharing the document with collaborators also shares the compound dictionary. This is by design — it allows collaborators to use ChemRef with the same compound data. No additional data sharing occurs.

Children's Privacy

ChemRef is a professional productivity tool and does not knowingly collect any information from children or any other users.

Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy is updated, the revised version will be posted at this URL with an updated date. Since ChemRef collects no data, substantive changes are unlikely.
